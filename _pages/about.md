Jon Wild is a settler scholar born and living in amiskwaciywâskahikan (Edmonton) currently
pursuing a dual Masters of Library and Information Studies and Digital Humanities at the
University of Alberta in amiskwacîy-wâskahikan. His research interests centre around activist
and counter-archives, data feminism, the politics of memory, and the poetic web. He is currently
writing his thesis on social and political movement archives in Alberta, and how dominant
professional practices reinforce archival silences around protest and activism in the province. He is very interested in exploring how archival and historical narratives and stories are created and reinforced.

Committed to informational and data justice, he has participated in social movements and
research projects related to police militarization and surveillance, Palestine solidarity, and
community knowledge mobilization. Since 2024, he has worked at University of Alberta Libraries
as a student information services assistant. He serves as a volunteer in numerous student organizations, and
in community archives such as the Alberta Labour History Institute.
